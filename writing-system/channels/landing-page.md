# Channel: Landing page

> **Status:** v1. Format rules for service / offer / sales pages. Voice, audience, kill list are loaded separately.

---

## Landing page skeleton

Compress for short pages. Expand for long-form sales pages. Never invert the section order.

1. **Hero.** Headline + subhead + primary CTA. Headline names the audience-state (the reader recognizes themselves). Subhead names the contrast move. CTA is the single next action.
2. **Problem framing.** Where the reader sees themselves. Pull verbatim from `audience/executives.md`. The reader should think *"that's exactly what I said on my last call with [coach / board / spouse]."*
3. **Anti-pattern (what they've tried).** Notion. EOS. Time-blocking. One or two failed EA hires. Name what's been tried. Honor the effort. Then explain why each failed *structurally*, not because the reader was lazy.
4. **The move.** Not a feature list. The structural shift: human-plus-system + replacement guarantee. Three components, max.
5. **Proof / receipts.** Specific stories with real names, numbers, dollars, timelines. Avoid vague testimonials.
6. **Pricing / packaging clarity.** What it costs, what's included, what's not, what to expect in onboarding. Don't hide.
7. **Objection handling (FAQ).** Lift from universal objections in `audience/executives.md`: replacement, English / accent, caliber, time zone / hours, NDA / confidentiality. 5–7 questions. Phrased as the audience phrases them.
8. **Final CTA.** Direct, single action. Restate the central promise above the button. Match the hero CTA.

## Hero patterns

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience-state + contrast** | *"You're [audience-state]. You [should be / shouldn't have to] [contrast]."* | *"You're the senior-most person in your company. You're still doing the most junior work."* |
| **Identity-preservation** | *"You built this. Now you deserve to be [verb that honors the founder identity]."* | *"You built this company. Now you deserve to be managed."* |
| **Outcome-state** | *"[Specific emotional payoff]. With [structural move]."* | *"Sleep without the ledger running. Human-plus-system, replacement built in."* |

The hero is not a tagline. It's the audience seeing themselves in the first line and seeing the move in the second.

---

## Length defaults

| Type | Word count |
|---|---|
| Service overview page | 600–1000 |
| Long-form sales page | 1500–3500 |
| Single-offer / waitlist page | 300–600 |
| Hero alone | 30–80 |

The reader leaves at the first generic sentence. Cut anything that doesn't earn the next scroll.

---

## Landing-page-specific format rules

- One CTA per page section. Don't compete with yourself.
- Hero CTA and final CTA must match. No bait-and-switch.
- Pricing section is *direct*, not buried. If the audience has to hunt for it, they bounce.
- FAQ uses the audience's actual question phrasing, not a sanitized version.
- Proof section uses real names (with permission) and specific receipts. Anonymous *"a client"* with no detail signals weakness.

## Examples: in-voice vs. out-of-voice

### Hero
- **OUT:** *"Executive Assistants for Modern Founders"*
- **IN:** *"You're the senior-most person in your company. You're still doing the most junior work. We can fix that."*

### Subhead
- **OUT:** *"Save time and grow your business with our world-class executive assistant solutions."*
- **IN:** *"Human-plus-system, run by someone we vetted, replaced if it doesn't work. You stop running the executive support function. We do."*

### Problem framing opener
- **OUT:** *"Many founders struggle with time management and operational overhead…"*
- **IN:** *"Six months ago you wrote down what your week was supposed to look like. It hasn't looked like that since. Notion has the system. You have the team. You're still the dam."*

### Proof
- **OUT:** *"We've helped hundreds of clients achieve their goals through our innovative platform…"*
- **IN:** *"Patric was about to hire another US-based EA at $90K. We placed his replacement in 11 days at a quarter of the cost. His team didn't notice the handoff."*

### Pullback above final CTA
- **OUT:** *"Take your business to the next level."*
- **IN:** *"You built this. What you deserve next isn't more hours back. It's a structural change in how the executive support function works, so you can be the leader you already are, without running the engine room every Tuesday at 11pm."*
