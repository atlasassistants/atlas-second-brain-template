# Channel: LinkedIn post

> **Status:** v1. Format rules for LinkedIn posts. Generated by the `repurposing` skill from a long-form source, or written standalone.

---

## LinkedIn post skeleton

1. **Hook line (first line).** Must stand alone. LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters with a *"see more"* button. If your hook isn't in that first line, nobody clicks.
2. **Personal narrative setup (line 2 onward).** *"I [did/learned/saw something specific]. Here's what it taught me."* Frame the post as a first-person story even if the source piece was more analytical.
3. **The lesson / the move.** Build the insight from the specific story. Concrete examples, real numbers, named subjects.
4. **The philosophical pullback.** One short paragraph at the close. Compressed version of the long-form pullback. Reframes what the tactic means for the reader's identity / leadership / business.
5. **Optional CTA.** Soft. *"Curious how others are thinking about this — drop a thought below."* OR no explicit CTA at all (the engagement comes from the post itself).
6. **Link in first comment if needed.** Never in the post body. LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes external links.

---

## Length defaults

| Type | Character count |
|---|---|
| Standard LinkedIn post | 1300–2000 |
| LinkedIn article (long-form, separate format) | 1500+ |
| Quick observation / hot take | 500–1000 |

Sweet spot is 1300–2000 chars for regular posts. Anything longer should be a LinkedIn article.

---

## Hook line patterns

The first line is everything. LinkedIn truncates at ~210 chars; if the hook doesn't land there, the post dies.

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Personal observation** | *"I was wrong about every productivity tool I bought in the last five years."* |
| **Specific receipt** | *"In 11 days, we placed Patric's EA replacement at one quarter the cost of his last hire."* |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | *"Two years ago, I couldn't write a single line of code. This week, I'm teaching a room of executives to build their own tools."* |
| **Contrarian observation** | *"Most founders I meet say they're the bottleneck. The ones who actually fix it do the opposite of what they assume."* |
| **Reframe of common belief** | *"For years, I thought hiring an EA was about delegation. I was solving the wrong problem."* |

Hook patterns that DON'T work on LinkedIn: lowercase contrast hooks (read as too casual for the platform), authority-moment (LinkedIn audience isn't pre-warmed by an authority figure the way email warm leads are), curiosity question (works better in X).

---

## LinkedIn-specific format rules

- **Sentence case for everything.** No lowercase casual (that's X energy).
- **Short paragraphs.** One idea per paragraph. White space is your friend on LinkedIn.
- **Use *"I"* heavily.** First-person narrative is what LinkedIn rewards. Most analytical claims should be reframed as *"I noticed,"* *"I learned,"* *"I built."*
- **No more than 3 hashtags at the end.** If used at all. Often: zero.
- **Links in the first comment, never in the body.** Algorithm penalty otherwise.
- **No em dashes.**
- **Bold can be used for emphasis on key phrases.** Sparingly.

---

## When this fires (vs. X thread / X short / newsletter)

LinkedIn fires when the user wants:
- A version of a long-form idea that reads as a personal narrative for a professional audience.
- A standalone post built around a specific moment / observation {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}} had.

LinkedIn does NOT fire for:
- Casual takes (those are X short).
- Step-by-step breakdowns (those are X thread).
- Long-form essays (that's a LinkedIn article, separate format — currently not implemented in this system).

## Examples: in-voice vs. out-of-voice

### Hook line
- **OUT:** *"In today's rapidly evolving business landscape, leveraging AI is no longer optional for founders looking to scale their operations effectively."*
- **IN:** *"For two years I told my team I'd hire an EA 'when things calm down.' Things never calmed down."*

### Body (the lesson built from a story)
- **OUT:** *"There are several key benefits to delegating effectively, including improved efficiency and enhanced strategic focus…"*
- **IN:** *"What I missed: every founder who actually got out of the bottleneck didn't optimize their time. They built the system that ran without them. It's not about being faster. It's about not being in the loop at all.*

*Patric figured this out before I did. He came to us with an EA he liked but couldn't scale. We placed his replacement in 11 days at one quarter the cost. His team didn't notice the handoff."*

### Pullback close
- **OUT:** *"In conclusion, every founder should consider leveraging the power of AI and quality executive support to take their business to the next level. Reach out to learn more!"*
- **IN:** *"The version of you who needed to do this work to know it was being done isn't the version of you the company needs in year three. Honoring the founder identity sometimes looks like getting out of the founder's seat in the parts of the business that no longer require you.*

*Curious how others are thinking about this transition. Drop a comment if you've crossed this gap recently."*
