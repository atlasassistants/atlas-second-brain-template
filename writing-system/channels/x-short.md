# Channel: X short

> **Status:** v1. Format rules for single-tweet posts (or 2–3 tweet micro-threads). Generated by the `repurposing` skill from a long-form source, or written standalone.

---

## What X short is

A standalone tweet (or 2–3 tweet setup-punchline) that captures one idea cleanly. Not a thread. Not a teaser. The post is the unit.

The most-quotable lines from a long-form piece become X shorts. The contrast moves. The receipts. The aphorisms.

---

## X short skeleton

### Single-tweet format (most common)
- 280 characters max.
- One complete idea. Hook + landing.
- Optional line breaks for rhythm.

### Two-tweet setup-punchline
- Tweet 1: setup (the observation, the question, the contrarian premise).
- Tweet 2: punchline (the receipt, the reframe, the implication).

### Three-tweet micro-thread
- Tweet 1: hook.
- Tweet 2: the move.
- Tweet 3: the philosophical pullback compressed.

Use micro-thread sparingly. If it needs more than 3 tweets, it's an X thread (see `channels/x-thread.md`).

---

## What works on X short

| Hook formula | Why it works as standalone |
|---|---|
| Contrast | Single-line punch. *"you don't need another tool. you need to be managed."* |
| Specific receipt | Quote-tweetable proof. *"Patric replaced his US EA in 11 days at 1/4 the cost."* |
| Replacement | Concrete before/after. *"replaced $8k/mo content team with one folder of .md files."* |
| Curiosity question | Provokes thought. *"what would your week one look like if your operations person left tomorrow?"* |
| Aphorism / observation | Lifted from the philosophical pullback. *"the bottleneck isn't the work. it's the permission you give yourself to stop being the executor."* |

What doesn't work as X short: authority-moment (needs context), confessional opening (needs setup), behind-the-scenes (needs body).

---

## Per-tweet rules

- Lowercase by default.
- No hashtags.
- No em dashes.
- No exclamation points.
- Line breaks for rhythm if helpful, not as default.
- One emoji max as signoff. Often: none.
- Link in a reply, never in the main tweet.

---

## Examples: in-voice vs. out-of-voice

### Single-tweet, contrast hook
- **OUT:** *"Want to scale your business? You need to invest in productivity tools that unlock your potential 🚀 #productivity #founders"*
- **IN:** *"you don't need another tool. you need to be managed.\n\nthe founders i know who got out of the bottleneck stopped optimizing their own time and started designing the system that runs without them."*

### Single-tweet, receipt
- **OUT:** *"We've helped many clients save time and money on their operations!"*
- **IN:** *"a healthcare CEO i taught replaced four months of his team's policy work in four days.\n\nthen pre-sold a custom tech stack to a competitor before building it.\n\nthe gap between idea and shipped just collapsed."*

### Setup-punchline (2 tweets)
- **Tweet 1 (setup):** *"every founder i meet says 'i'm the bottleneck.'"*
- **Tweet 2 (punchline):** *"the ones who fix it stop trying to optimize their own time and start designing the system that runs without them. it's not about being faster. it's about not being in the loop at all."*
