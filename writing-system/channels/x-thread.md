# Channel: X thread

> **Status:** v1. Format rules for serial threads on X. Generated by the `repurposing` skill from a long-form source.

---

## X thread skeleton

5–10 tweets typical. Up to 20 for high-density topics. Each tweet a complete idea.

1. **Hook tweet (tweet 1).** Must stand alone. Most readers see only this one. Use a contrarian, receipt, replacement, or curiosity hook from `engine/hooks.md`. Lowercase casual.
2. **Setup tweet (tweet 2).** The contrast or stakes. Why the reader should keep reading. *"here's what most people miss"* and similar AI tics from `kill-list.md` are banned — find the actual reason.
3. **Body tweets (tweets 3–N).** One idea per tweet. Numbered steps work. Concrete examples per tweet. Specific receipts beat compressed abstractions.
4. **Close tweet (final).** Either a compressed philosophical pullback (1 tweet, very tight) OR a soft prompt (*"more like this in [link in reply]"*).
5. **Self-reply with link.** Optional. If linking to a longer piece (newsletter, X article), put the link in a reply to your own thread, never in the main tweets. The X algorithm penalizes external links in main threads.

---

## Per-tweet rules

- **280 character max per tweet** (X long-form tweets up to 25,000 chars exist but defeat the thread format).
- **Lowercase by default.** Title case reads as marketing.
- **Line breaks between thoughts.** Never dense paragraphs in a tweet.
- **No hashtags ever.** Hashtag spam pattern. {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}}'s voice doesn't do this.
- **No em dashes.** Confirmed kill (see `kill-list.md`).
- **Specific receipts in tweets.** *"Patric replaced his EA in 11 days"* beats *"clients see fast results."*
- **One emoji at most per thread, used as a signoff or section break.** Never sprinkled in body tweets.

---

## Hook tweet patterns (tweet 1)

- **Contrarian:** *"you don't need a content team. you need 30 markdown files."*
- **Replacement:** *"i replaced my $8k/mo content stack with one folder."*
- **Specific receipt:** *"Patric replaced his US EA in 11 days at 1/4 the cost."*
- **Behind-the-scenes:** *"i run {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}}'s ai strategy and i couldn't write a line of code two years ago."*
- **Step promise:** *"here's how i went from drowning in inbox to a 4-hour CEO week, in 3 steps:"*

Hook patterns that DON'T work for tweet 1: authority-moment (too long for 280), curiosity question (better for X short).

## Body tweet patterns (tweets 2–N)

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| **Numbered step** | *"3) every skill in your system needs to consult the resolver before writing. otherwise it invents its own filing logic."* |
| **Anti-pattern → move** | *"most operators stop at 'do this.' the ones who actually shift their business stop, then ask what doing this means about who they're becoming."* |
| **Specific receipt** | *"in 30 days my agent processed 200 inputs daily. zero misfilings. that's after 10 of 13 skills had hardcoded paths."* |
| **Quote / aphorism** | *"a routing table that learns from its own traffic. that's the endgame."* |

---

## Anti-patterns to kill (specific to X threads)

- **Re-using the long-form opener verbatim as the hook tweet.** Reformatting, not rethinking. See `engine/repurpose.md`.
- **Numbering tweets *"1/N", "2/N"* in the body.** X auto-numbers. Don't add manual numbers unless they serve content (numbered steps).
- **Cliffhangers without payoff** (*"this changes everything (continued)…"*). Manipulation. {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}}'s audience walks away.
- **Hashtags.** Banned.
- **Emoji-walled tweets** (every tweet starts with 🚀 or similar). Banned.
- **Long tweets that should have been multiple tweets.** If a tweet is 270 characters and dense, split it. The thread format rewards rhythm.

## Examples: in-voice vs. out-of-voice

### Hook tweet
- **OUT:** *"In today's competitive business landscape, founders need to leverage AI to optimize their workflows. Here's a thread on how to do it right 🧵👇"*
- **IN:** *"you don't need another tool. you need to be managed.\n\nhere's why every productivity stack {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}}'s clients tried before us failed, and what actually worked:"*

### Body tweet
- **OUT:** *"Tip 3: Implement systems thinking into your operations to unlock exponential growth potential."*
- **IN:** *"3) every operator who got out of their bottleneck did the same thing first: stopped trying to optimize their own time, started designing the system that runs without them."*

### Close tweet
- **OUT:** *"Hope this helps! Drop a like if you learned something 💪"*
- **IN:** *"the founders i know who actually crossed this gap don't talk about productivity anymore.\n\nthey talk about how their week feels different.\n\nthat's the real metric."*
