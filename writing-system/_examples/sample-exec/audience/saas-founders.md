# SaaS Founders (Primary)

## Who they are

Founders or founder-engineers running B2B SaaS startups, typically Series A through Series B (5-50 employees), often technical, often the original architect of the product. Frequently ICs who jumped into the founder seat without managerial experience, now scaling a team and a codebase simultaneously.

## Pain

- Engineering velocity has dropped as the team grew, but no one can name where the time is going
- Their AI tooling is fragmented across 5-10 tools with no compounding leverage
- Decisions about infrastructure (build vs. buy, framework choice, what to abstract) feel like bets without enough data
- Hiring senior engineers but losing them in the first 90 days
- Can't tell whether their product team is shipping the right things — they ship a lot, but conversion isn't moving

## What they currently rely on

- Common tooling stacks (Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, in-house scripts) bolted together
- Monthly all-hands metrics that show output but don't surface bottlenecks
- Gut-check decisions about architecture trade-offs

## Why current solutions fail them

- The bolted-together stack means knowledge doesn't compound across tools
- Output metrics measure motion, not throughput — they hide the stuck-points
- Architectural gut-checks work for the founder but don't transfer to the team they're hiring

## What they're skeptical of

- AI hype cycles (every quarter brings a new model that "changes everything")
- Productivity-tool think-pieces from people who haven't shipped
- Frameworks marketed as universal that the author hasn't actually used at their stage

## What they want

- A way to see where engineering velocity is actually leaking — concrete, weekly
- An AI tooling stack where the parts compound (output of one tool feeds the next)
- A hiring rubric for senior engineers that produces fewer 90-day exits
- Architecture decision frameworks that survive the next hire

## What they fear

- They missed the AI productivity inflection and their competitor caught it
- They've built a culture where the team only ships what they (the founder) explicitly asks for, and they can't scale past that
- They're not actually a founder — they're an engineer with a title
- The next hire is a senior person who walks because the tooling is too messy
