# IC to Leadership Transitioners (Secondary)

## Who they are

Senior individual contributors (IC4-IC6 / staff-to-principal) who have been promoted into management or are about to be. 5-12 years of IC depth; new to the leadership track. Often hesitant about whether the transition is the right call.

## Pain

- They can build the thing, but reviewing other people's builds doesn't feel like work
- Their manager wants them to "be more strategic" but won't say what that means concretely
- They're losing depth in their craft (they used to ship daily; now they ship weekly at best)
- Their new direct reports test them: "Why should I take direction from you?"
- Performance reviews feel arbitrary and they can't tell if they're doing well

## What they currently rely on

- Pattern-matching from past managers (mostly bad ones, hence the IC career so far)
- Engineering management Twitter / blogs
- One-on-ones with their own manager who's also figuring it out

## Why current solutions fail them

- Pattern-matching from bad managers reproduces bad patterns
- Engineering management Twitter is heavy on opinion, light on receipts
- Their manager isn't ahead of them on the curve; they need someone 3-5 steps ahead

## What they're skeptical of

- "Servant leadership" / "vibes-based leadership" frameworks that have no measurable output
- Promotion frameworks designed for FAANG, applied to startups
- Anyone who claims management is "easy if you care about people"

## What they want

- A clear definition of what "strategic" means at their level, with concrete examples
- A hiring/firing rubric that produces a team they're proud of in 12 months
- A way to keep enough technical depth that they're respected by ICs
- Confidence that the transition was the right call

## What they fear

- They become a paperwork manager — meetings, OKRs, no shipped artifacts
- Their team underperforms and they can't tell why
- They lose their craft and end up unemployable as an IC if the manager track doesn't work out
- Their direct reports don't respect them
