# Content Strategy — Cadence

> **Status:** Populated for Sarah Chen / Gridline. Per-domain cadence targets ~24 long-form pieces/year across three domains on a bi-weekly newsletter schedule. D1 carries the heaviest volume; D3 the lightest.

This file sets the publishing cadence per domain and validates that the total production load is sustainable for a founder operating at Series A with limited writing bandwidth.

---

## Cadence per domain

| Domain | Cadence | Per year | Mode | Funnel role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **D1 — Engineering Velocity at Scale** | ~every 5 weeks | 10 | Scheduled | Top-of-funnel → Gridline awareness |
| **D2 — AI-Native Infrastructure** | ~every 6–7 weeks | 8 | Scheduled | Bottom-of-funnel → Gridline demo/trial |
| **D3 — Founder-as-Builder** | ~every 9 weeks | 6 | Opportunistic | Personal brand → newsletter list growth |

**Total: 24 long-form pieces/year.**

---

## Production load math

- **"From The Build" newsletter:** 24 issues/year ≈ bi-weekly cadence (1 issue every ~2 weeks). Matches Sarah's stated rhythm.
- **Domain interleave:** In any given 6-week window, Sarah publishes approximately 2 D1, 1–2 D2, and 1 D3 piece. The bi-weekly newsletter mixes all three rather than running domain-blocks.
- **Total long-form per year:** 24 pieces (single newsletter + LinkedIn chain; no separate EA or secondary list).
- **Plus repurposing chain** (see [[distribution]]): each long-form piece may spawn a LinkedIn long-form and, for D1 and D2, occasional X threads. D3 repurposes to LinkedIn only.

---

## Why this weighting (D1 > D2 > D3)

**D1 at 10/year.** Engineering velocity is Sarah's highest-volume material well — it's the broadest domain and has the most sub-topics (three pillars × multiple angles per pillar). It's also the most evergreen: a D1 piece on handoff design compounds in discoverability over time. High cadence is sustainable here.

**D2 at 8/year.** AI-native infrastructure is Gridline's positioning, which means these pieces need to be right — specific, technically credible, and earned by something Sarah has actually shipped or decided. Rushing to 10/year risks the receipts becoming thin. 8/year keeps the bar where it belongs.

**D3 at 6/year.** Founder-as-builder pieces require Sarah to have had an experience she's ready to analyze in public. These can't be scheduled in the same way — they come from real moments (a hire that taught her something, a build she did herself that she shouldn't have). Opportunistic-mode, not purely scheduled. Six pieces/year is approximately one every 8–9 weeks; realistic given the material constraint.

---

## The bi-weekly interleave

"From The Build" ships every two weeks. Across 26 issues/year, 24 of them carry a new long-form piece; 2 or so are special issues (retrospective, announcement, or off-cycle). Domain rotation might look like:

```
Issue 1:  D1 — velocity leak detection
Issue 2:  D2 — AI tool compounding
Issue 3:  D1 — team handoff design
Issue 4:  D3 — ic-to-founder transition
Issue 5:  D1 — tech debt budgeting
Issue 6:  D2 — agent harness design
Issue 7:  D1 — velocity leak detection (new angle)
Issue 8:  D2 — infra decision rubrics
...
```

No strict rotation required — the cadence targets are annual, not per-issue. But the newsletter should not run more than 3 consecutive issues in the same domain without breaking to another.

---

## Sustainability check

- **24 long-form pieces/year** is approximately 1 every two weeks — the rhythm a founder can sustain if the writing-system handles drafting and Sarah handles final voice/judgment/edit.
- **D3's opportunistic mode is the load-management valve.** If Sarah is in a high-execution sprint and material is thin, D3 cadence can slip to 4/year without breaking the newsletter rhythm — D1 and D2 carry the volume.
- **D2's quality ceiling matters more than its volume.** Better to publish 6 credible D2 pieces than 8 where two are thin on receipt. If material is weak in a given period, replace a D2 slot with a D1 piece rather than force a D2 publish.
