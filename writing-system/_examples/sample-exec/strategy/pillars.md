# Content Strategy — Pillars

> **Status:** Populated for Sarah Chen / Gridline. Nine pillars across three domains (3 per domain). Designed against Sarah's material well and content history.

A **pillar** is a recurring sub-topic inside a domain — a stable content angle Sarah can target for multiple long-form pieces. Pillars sit below domains and above specific topics/ideas.

Each domain has **three pillars.** Slugs are canonical — use them verbatim in draft frontmatter (`pillar:` field) and when invoking topic-suggestion or research skills.

## D1 — Engineering Velocity at Scale

| # | Pillar | Slug | Definition |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **Velocity Leak Detection** | `velocity-leak-detection` | How to find where a growing engineering team is bleeding shipping speed — the recurring structural patterns that kill throughput (not the one-off fires). |
| P2 | **Team Handoff Design** | `team-handoff-design` | The contracts that exist (or don't) between engineers, between teams, between eng and product — and why bad handoff design is the most common source of compounding slowdown at 15–30 people. |
| P3 | **Tech Debt Budgeting** | `tech-debt-budgeting` | Not a plea to pay down debt. A system for deciding how much debt is load-bearing and how much is dragging — and what the actual budget number is for a team at each stage of growth. |

**Pillar design notes.**
- P1 covers *diagnosis* — where is velocity going? What are the structural culprits?
- P2 covers *process design* — the handoff layer is where most velocity leaks originate.
- P3 covers *tradeoff management* — Sarah's view that tech debt isn't always bad; the discipline is in the budget, not the elimination.

## D2 — AI-Native Infrastructure

| # | Pillar | Slug | Definition |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **AI Tool Compounding** | `ai-tool-compounding` | What it means for AI tools to compound — each tool's output feeds the next, rather than adding isolated surface area. How to evaluate a toolchain for compounding potential before you commit. |
| P2 | **Agent Harness Design** | `agent-harness-design` | The engineering work of giving AI agents well-defined jobs in the build system — what the harness looks like, what breaks when it's missing, and why harness quality determines whether agents amplify or create noise. |
| P3 | **Infra Decision Rubrics** | `infra-decision-rubrics` | The actual decision frameworks Sarah uses when the engineering team faces an AI infrastructure call — build vs. buy, embed vs. wrap, when to bet on a new tool vs. when to wait. |

**Pillar design notes.**
- P1 covers *evaluation* — how do you judge AI tools before you're locked in?
- P2 covers *engineering craft* — harness design as a first-class engineering discipline.
- P3 covers *decision-making* — the rubrics that make hard calls legible under uncertainty.

## D3 — Founder-as-Builder

| # | Pillar | Slug | Definition |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | **IC-to-Founder Transition** | `ic-to-founder-transition` | The specific crossing from individual contributor to founder-CEO: what you gain, what you lose, and the ways the mental model you built as an engineer breaks when you run the company. |
| P2 | **Keeping Craft While Scaling** | `keeping-craft-while-scaling` | The ongoing practice of staying technically credible as the company grows — when staying hands-on is a compounding asset and when it becomes a delegation failure. |
| P3 | **Founder Hiring Instinct** | `founder-hiring-instinct` | How a technical founder evaluates engineering candidates and leaders when they're no longer writing production code themselves — the signals that survive abstraction and the ones that don't. |

**Pillar design notes.**
- P1 covers *the crossing itself* — the moment, the transition, the disorientation.
- P2 covers *the ongoing practice* — this isn't a one-time transition; it's a recurrent negotiation at every growth stage.
- P3 covers *the downstream hiring consequence* — once you've crossed, how do you maintain technical judgment when assessing the people who build what you no longer build?
