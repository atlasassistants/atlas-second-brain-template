# Content Strategy — Audience-Domain Mapping

> **Status:** Populated for Sarah Chen / Gridline. Domain-to-audience matrix locked. D3 is Sarah's cross-audience domain — both audiences are genuine primary readers. Pairing rule: mixed by piece, with specific D1/D3 pairing moments.

This file maps which audience consumes which domain, and resolves how the writing-system handles the relationship between D1/D2 (founder-focused) and D3 (dual-audience) in production.

---

## Domain → audience matrix

| Domain | Primary audience | Channel | Secondary audience | Cross-pollination |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| D1 — Engineering Velocity at Scale | **SaaS founders** (founder-engineers) | "From The Build" newsletter + LinkedIn | IC-to-leadership | Moderate — ICs who are 1–2 years from founding or stepping up read D1 as aspirational reference |
| D2 — AI-Native Infrastructure | **SaaS founders** (founder-engineers) | "From The Build" newsletter + LinkedIn | Light (staff-to-TL crossings) | Light — D2 infrastructure calls are primarily founder/exec-level decisions |
| D3 — Founder-as-Builder | **SaaS founders + IC-to-leadership (dual)** | "From The Build" newsletter + LinkedIn | — | None needed — both are genuine primary readers |

**D3 is Sarah's cross-audience domain.** This is unusual. Most domains have one genuine audience; D3 has two, and they read it for different reasons:
- **SaaS founders** read D3 as ongoing craft — *"this is the crossing I already made, and I'm still negotiating it."*
- **IC-to-leadership transitioners** read D3 as a map — *"this is the crossing I'm about to make, or just made."*

Both reads are real, not manufactured. Sarah should write D3 with both readers in mind — not alternating between them (wrong), but writing at the level where both recognize themselves.

---

## The pairing rule (D1 ↔ D3 in production)

The connection between D1 (Engineering Velocity) and D3 (Founder-as-Builder) creates a natural production question: when a D1 piece ships on team handoffs or velocity leaks, is there a companion D3 piece on how the founder's role shifts when they're diagnosing these problems rather than fixing them directly?

**Production rule: mixed by piece.**

- **Default: independent.** Most D1 and D3 pieces ship independently. The conceptual relationship is scaffolding, not a production constraint.
- **Selective pairing.** A small subset of pieces is deliberately paired when the topic has both a structural lens (D1) and a founder-craft lens (D3) that genuinely illuminate each other.
- **Never reframed.** The same piece is not rewritten for both audiences. Reuse happens at the *receipt / example* level, not the piece level.

### When to pair deliberately

Reach for D1/D3 pairing when:

1. **The same engineering decision has a system-design angle and a founder-instinct angle.** *"Here's how we designed handoffs at Gridline at 20 people"* (D1) + *"Here's how I knew when to stop being the one who designed them"* (D3). Same event, different lens.
2. **A growth stage inflection point reveals both the org problem and the personal cost.** Scaling from 10 to 25 engineers generates D1 content (what breaks) and D3 content (what the founder's identity has to give up).
3. **Sarah makes a hiring decision that reveals something about engineering velocity AND founder hiring instinct simultaneously.** The hire who changed how the team shipped (D1) is also a receipt in the D3 story about trusting instinct you can no longer verify with code.

### When NOT to pair

- D2 content. AI infrastructure decisions are primarily founder-level; there's no clean IC-to-leadership companion in Sarah's domain design. Don't manufacture one.
- Time-sensitive D1 receipts. If Sarah just shipped a velocity improvement at Gridline, publish the D1 piece. Don't wait for the D3 angle to materialize.
- D3 pieces that are personal-craft without an engineering-system counterpart. Not every D3 piece generates a D1 companion; the transition essay that has no system-design corollary ships alone.

---

## D2 and audience: direct-path, no pairing

D2 (AI-Native Infrastructure) serves SaaS founders only. Infrastructure decisions at the level Sarah writes about (harness design, tool compounding, decision rubrics) are founder/exec calls, not IC-level decisions. The secondary IC-to-leadership audience reads D2 incidentally — staff engineers stepping into tech lead roles start facing these decisions — but it's not a pairing domain.

**No D2/D3 pairing rule.** If a D2 topic has a personal-craft angle, and the angle is interesting, that's a standalone D3 piece, not a paired moment.

---

## Implications for production load

- Sarah's "From The Build" newsletter is the primary distribution vehicle for all three domains.
- D3's dual-audience design means Sarah can speak to both audiences in a single newsletter issue without needing to run two separate lists. The "From The Build" subscriber base contains both founders and IC-to-leadership readers.
- If Sarah ever segments her list, D3 content is what she'd keep on the combined list; D2 is the most likely candidate for a separate deep-technical sub-list.
- D1 and D2 share the same primary audience but have different commercial roles (see [[commercial-mapping]]). Production scheduling should interleave them, not cluster all D1 then all D2.
