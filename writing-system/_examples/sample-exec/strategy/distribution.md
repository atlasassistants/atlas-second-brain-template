# Content Strategy — Distribution

> **Status:** Populated for Sarah Chen / Gridline. Maps which channels each domain ships to and the repurposing pattern per domain. D1 and D2 run full chains; D3 is newsletter + LinkedIn only.

This file specifies which channels each domain reaches and how the long-form source piece repurposes (or doesn't) into shorter formats.

---

## Channels Sarah runs

- **"From The Build" newsletter** — bi-weekly, primary distribution vehicle for all three domains
- **LinkedIn long-form** — 600–1000 word adapted pieces; exec and founder audience on LinkedIn
- **X (Twitter) threads** — tactical breakdowns of D1 and D2 content; Sarah's X audience skews technical founder
- **X long-form articles** — occasional full essays on X for D2 content where the technical depth warrants it
- **Gated content / landing pages** — selective use for D2 (infrastructure guides, decision frameworks as lead-gen for Gridline)

## Per-domain notes

### D1 — Engineering Velocity at Scale → Full chain (no gating)

Engineering velocity content is the most thread-friendly of Sarah's three domains. *"Three places where handoff design breaks at 20 engineers."* *"The receipt: our cycle time went from 6 days to 3 after we changed this."* These compress well.

- **Newsletter ("From The Build")** — primary destination, full long-form piece.
- **LinkedIn long-form** — always; adapted (tighter opening, slightly shorter) for the LinkedIn reader. Gridline's ICP is on LinkedIn.
- **X thread** — always; distilled to 6–10 posts on the core structural argument. D1 content threads well because the pillars (velocity leaks, handoff design, tech debt) are naturally enumerable.
- **No X long-form.** D1 pieces don't need the full-essay X treatment — the thread captures the value.
- **No gated content.** D1 is top-of-funnel brand content; gating kills distribution at this stage.

### D2 — AI-Native Infrastructure → Full chain + selective gating

AI-native infrastructure content is the most technically specific, which makes it both X-article-worthy and lead-gen-eligible. The infrastructure decision frameworks and harness design guides have durable utility — they're worth gating for Gridline list growth.

- **Newsletter ("From The Build")** — primary destination.
- **LinkedIn long-form** — always; adapted for the LinkedIn reader. D2 content on LinkedIn positions Sarah as a category-creator, not just a founder.
- **X thread** — selective. Not every D2 piece distills well; pick the pieces where the argument has a clean step-by-step structure (e.g., *"how to evaluate an AI toolchain in 3 moves"*).
- **X long-form** — always. D2 pieces are the most technically substantive and earn full-essay treatment on X. The Gridline CTA sits naturally at the end of an X long-form.
- **Gated content / landing page** — selective. Decision rubrics, harness design templates, or infrastructure evaluation frameworks make strong gated offers for Gridline list-building or trial conversion. Not every D2 piece earns a gate — only the ones with standalone utility beyond the essay format.

### D3 — Founder-as-Builder → Newsletter + LinkedIn only

Founder-craft content is personal and narrative. It doesn't compress well into threads, and Sarah's X audience for this type of content is thin relative to LinkedIn. Save the repurposing cost.

- **Newsletter ("From The Build")** — primary destination, full piece.
- **LinkedIn long-form** — always; this is the right format and right audience for D3 content. Founders and engineering leaders who would resonate with D3 are on LinkedIn in long-form mode.
- **No X distribution.** D3 content on X either goes too short (loses the nuance) or requires a full essay (too infrequent for Sarah's X posting rhythm). Default off.
- **No gating.** D3 is brand-building; it should travel freely.

---

## Repurposing pattern observations

- **D1 and D2 share LinkedIn long-form as a constant.** The LinkedIn audience is the primary commercial audience — SaaS founders and engineering leaders. Both domains should always repurpose to LinkedIn.
- **D3 has the narrowest chain.** This is by design. Sarah's D3 content is personal-craft and narrative; forcing it into thread or gated formats wastes effort and dilutes the voice.
- **D2 is the only domain with gated content potential.** The infrastructure decision frameworks and rubrics in D2 have standalone utility as resources. This makes D2 the primary channel for Gridline list-building and lead-gen beyond newsletter subscription.

## Production-load implications

Per long-form source piece, repurposing chain output:

| Domain | Outputs per piece | Annual repurposed output |
|---|---|---|
| D1 | Newsletter + LinkedIn + X thread (3 outputs) | ~30 outputs/year (10 × 3) |
| D2 | Newsletter + LinkedIn + X long-form + selective thread + selective gated (3–5 outputs) | ~27–40 outputs/year (8 × 3–5) |
| D3 | Newsletter + LinkedIn (2 outputs) | ~12 outputs/year (6 × 2) |

**Total annual content output across all formats: ~70–80 published items.** Sustainable for a founder-led content operation with writing-system support.
