# Sample Exec: Sarah Chen / Gridline

**This is a fictional exec. Read these files as a *demonstration of shape*, not a *target to mimic*.**

The voice, audience, kill-list, and strategy here are populated examples of what `/voice-onboard` and `/content-strategy-onboard` produce when run end-to-end. They show what good population looks like for the writing-system. They are not Atlas's real exec content, and your exec should not look like Sarah unless their domain happens to overlap.

## Sarah's profile

- **Role:** Founder + CEO
- **Company:** Gridline — B2B SaaS, AI-native infrastructure for product engineering teams
- **Stage:** Series A, ~25 employees
- **Audience primary:** Founder-engineers and IC-to-leadership transitioners
- **Audience secondary:** Engineering managers at growth-stage startups
- **Voice in one line:** Direct, builder-tone, anti-jargon, anchors arguments in shipped product
- **Publishing rhythm:** Bi-weekly founders' newsletter ("From The Build") + occasional LinkedIn long-form + sporadic X threads

## How to read these files

1. Open [[voice]] first — see the voice signature shape
2. Open [[audience/saas-founders]] — see a populated ICP
3. Open [[strategy/domains]] and [[strategy/pillars]] — see content-strategy outputs
4. Open [[drafts/sample-newsletter]] — see voice + strategy applied to a draft

If your exec's domain is unrelated to Gridline's, that's fine — the *shape* of the population (which fields, how detailed, how cross-referenced) is the takeaway, not Sarah's content itself.

## When to update this example

Don't. These files are immutable demonstrations. If you want to evolve them, fork them; don't modify in place.
