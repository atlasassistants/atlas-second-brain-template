# Quick check-in — where are you on the AI tooling stack?

Subject: Quick check-in — where are you on the AI tooling stack?

---

Marcus —

Saw you hit 18 engineers last month. Congrats.

I want to ask you something directly, because I asked myself this at the same moment and got the answer wrong: are you still in the codebase enough to know whether your AI tooling is actually compounding, or are you trusting that it is because the output numbers look better?

The receipt for why I'm asking: at 18 people, I thought Gridline's AI tooling was working because engineers were visibly faster. What I didn't see — couldn't see from where I was sitting — was that none of the tool outputs were connected. Each tool was doing its job. The system wasn't compounding.

The thing that actually fixed it wasn't a new tool. It was writing down what job each agent was doing inside the build system and what format it handed off to the next step. Three hours of work. The compounding started the week after.

If you're seeing faster-looking individual output but the sprint-level throughput isn't moving with it, that's probably the pattern.

Worth a call if you want to walk through what we built. No agenda — just figured this was the moment I wished someone had flagged it for me.

— Sarah
