# Engine: CTA patterns

> **Status:** CTA library v1. Direct reply is the default for email and newsletter. Book-a-call is the default for landing pages. Match CTA to the reader's intent at the point of consumption.

A CTA isn't a button. It's a request. The right CTA matches the reader's energy at the moment they finish reading.

---

## The five CTA types

### 1. Direct reply

**Format:** *"Hit reply and tell me [specific question]. I read every single one."*

**When it works:** Most emails. All newsletters. Mid-funnel content.

**Why it works:** Low friction. Builds the relationship. Reply is a buying signal {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}} can act on.

**Examples:**
- *"Hit reply and tell me what your bottleneck looks like."*
- *"What's the one tool in your stack that costs the most and gives you the least? Reply and let me know."*
- *"What would week one look like if your operations person left tomorrow? Hit reply."*

**Rule:** Always pair with a *specific* question. Don't ask *"what do you think?"* That gets ignored.

---

### 2. Soft conditional

**Format:** *"If [condition that describes the reader's situation], [low-pressure next step]."*

**When it works:** Mid-funnel emails. Top of cold sequences. Pieces where you don't want to push but want to leave a door open.

**Examples:**
- *"If this sounds like you, hit reply."*
- *"If you've already tried two EA hires and it didn't work, the third time isn't going to be a different version of the same. Reply if that lands."*

---

### 3. Book a call

**Format:** *"Book a 30-minute call here."* / *"Book a call."*

**When it works:** Landing pages (default). Late-funnel emails. Hot leads. Bottom of long-form sales pages.

**Examples:**
- *"Book a 30-minute call. We'll show you what the start-of-day report looks like."*
- *"Ready to talk? Book a call here."*

**Rule:** Pair with a clear value preview ("we'll show you X" / "we'll diagnose Y"). Bare *"Book a call"* gets clicked less than *"Book a call to see the system in action."*

---

### 4. Curious open-loop

**Format:** A question that doesn't ask for action but plants a thought.

**When it works:** Top-of-list emails. Newsletter closings when the goal is reflection, not response. Pieces where the goal is to make the reader think, not click.

**Examples:**
- *"What would it look like if you stopped waking up at 3am running the ledger?"*
- *"What kind of leader are you when you stop being the executor?"*

**Rule:** Use sparingly. Open-loops without follow-up frustrate readers. If the reader actually engages with the question internally, that's the win.

---

### 5. Authority-mirroring

**Format:** *"If your [authority figure] sent you this, they're right. [Next step]."*

**When it works:** Referral-warmed emails. Top of pages targeting authority-channel traffic.

**Examples:**
- *"If your coach sent you this, they're right. Reply and we'll show you what's next."*
- *"If your fractional COO is reading this with you on a call, here's what to ask them."*

---

## Per-channel CTA defaults

| Channel | Primary CTA | Secondary CTAs |
|---|---|---|
| Email | Direct reply | Soft conditional, Book a call (hot leads only), Authority-mirroring |
| Landing page | Book a call | Direct-reply alternative, See pricing, See the system |
| Newsletter / X article | Direct reply (P.S.) | Curious open-loop, Soft pointer to product (sparingly) |
| X thread | Reply / RT / save | Link to long-form in self-reply |
| X short | Implicit (no CTA needed; the post is the unit) | Optional reply prompt |
| LinkedIn post | Reply / connect | Link to long-form (in first comment, not body) |

---

## Rules for CTA writing

1. **One CTA per piece.** Multiple CTAs split attention. Pick one. Sub-asks (P.S. reply prompts) don't count if they're conversational, not transactional.
2. **The hero CTA and the final CTA on a landing page must match.** No bait-and-switch.
3. **Specific over generic.** *"Hit reply and tell me what your bottleneck looks like"* beats *"What do you think?"*
4. **No exclamation points.** Conviction comes from sentence construction (see `kill-list.md`).
5. **No "Don't miss out!" / "Limited time only!" / "Act now!"** Cheap urgency. {{VAULT_OWNER_SHORT_NAME}}'s audience walks away from this language.
6. **Match CTA to funnel stage.** Direct reply for top-funnel. Book-a-call for bottom-funnel. Don't ask for a meeting on the first touch.
